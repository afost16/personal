Hop Keys

Role

Human Robot Interaction

UX Research

Team

3 members

Timeline

14 weeks

This past semester, my team designed HopKeys, an instructional robot frog designed to increase efficiency and enjoyability for young children when they first learn how to type. It's designed to help children learn touch typing, or typing based on muscle memory. HopKeys consists of two parts: the actual robot as well as a pair of gloves. The gloves have different colors on each finger that correspond to certain letters. Children will see the colored-letter on the robot screen and associate it which finger on their glove to type with. This helps them develop good typing fundamentals, with the intention to make future typing a better experience.

The project has been accepted for the 2024 HRI Student Design Challenge. This year's theme for the design challenge is "making the everyday interactive."

Much of my efforts were concentrated in the research submitted for the conference. This project allowed me to work with students in other discplines (both of which were computer science students), which was a nice change of pace. Working with robotics created a unique set of technical constraints and exposed me to physical forms of design.
