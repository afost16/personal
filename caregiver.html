Improving Co-Design Sessions

Role

Research Co-Lead

Team

2 members, was previously 8

Timeline

6 months and counting

As a researcher at Proactive Health Informatics, I initially became involved in a project as a pilot study participant for an HRI co-design session. The goal was to develop a robot to enhance the relationship between familial or friend caregivers and care recipients. After conducting a fully fledged co-design session, our team found ourselves directionless, prompting a shift in focus towards analyzing the interactions of the participants.

Currently, a PhD student and I are coding a recorded co-design session, with the ultimate aim to devise more effective approaches for future co-design sessions involving the caregiver and care recipient dyad.

This research is an ongoing endeavor to be submitted to the ACM Conference on Designing Interactive Systems 2024, specifically for the WIP (Work In Progress) & Provocation / Doctoral Consortium.

After submitting the reflection paper to the ACM, I will be leading my own research to design co-design sessions in hopes to validate the insights from our reflection.
